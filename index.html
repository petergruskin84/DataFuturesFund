Why Now

Compute is becoming a tradeable commodity.

For the first time, GPU compute is getting the institutional infrastructure that every major commodity eventually needs — standardized pricing, and now, futures markets.

CME × SILICON DATA

CME Group and Silicon Data announced plans in May 2026 to launch a first-in-class compute futures market, pending regulatory review, based on daily GPU rental-rate benchmarks.

ICE × ORNN

Intercontinental Exchange and Ornn separately announced plans for GPU compute futures contracts built on Ornn's Compute Price Index — a second major exchange entering the space.

EARLY INVESTOR INTEREST

Asset managers including ProShares and Rex Shares filed for exchange-traded products tied to the proposed contracts within days of the CME announcement.

Sources: CME Group & Silicon Data press release (May 2026); ICE & Ornn press release (May 2026); CNBC. Referenced for market context only — not an endorsement of the Fund.

The Approach

A disciplined, phased response to dislocation.

Rather than trying to time the bottom, the Fund's strategy is built around distinct phases of capital deployment — becoming more aggressive as dislocations deepen.

01

Early Dislocation

An initial, modest tranche deploys as the first signs of a meaningful price break appear — establishing early exposure without overcommitting capital.

02

Deep Value

The largest tranche is reserved for the point of maximum dislocation — where history suggests the most compelling long-term entry points tend to occur.

03

Reserve Capital

A final reserve stands ready for a truly extreme scenario — held in short-duration instruments in the meantime so it isn't sitting idle.

Specific trigger levels, allocation weights, and contract mechanics are detailed in the full whitepaper, available on request.
